Digital Assets

Bitcoin & Crypto Staking

Cryptocurrencies held for investment are subject to 2.5% Zakat on their market value. If you are staking coins, Zakat is due on both the principal and rewards earned.

Ref: AAOIFI Standard 35
Web3

Zakat on NFTs and Digital Art

If NFTs are purchased for resale, they are trading goods. 2.5% Zakat is due on the current floor price or estimated market value at the time of your Zakat anniversary.

Ref: Contemporary Fiqh
Bullion

Gold Jewelry and Ornaments

Zakat is obligatory on all gold jewelry if it reaches the Nisab (87.48g). Calculation is based on the current market rate on your specific Zakat date.

Ref: Hanafi Jurisprudence
Bullion

Silver Nisab and Assets

The Silver Nisab is 612.36 grams. If your total wealth exceeds the value of this much silver, you are liable for Zakat at a rate of 2.5%.

Ref: Mishkat al-Masabih
Social Finance

Zakat on Committees (BC)

Money paid into a committee but not received yet is an asset; pay Zakat on it. If already received, subtract remaining installments as debt.

Ref: Local Darul Ifta
Insurance

Life Insurance Policies

For conventional life insurance, Zakat is only due on the total premiums paid (principal). For Takaful, it is due on the surrender value.

Ref: Islamic Finance Council
Real Estate

Plots bought for Investment

If property is purchased with the intention of selling for profit, pay 2.5% Zakat on its current market value every year.

Ref: Fatawa Alamgiri
Real Estate

Zakat on Rental Income

No Zakat on the property value itself. Zakat is only due on accumulated rent money if it remains in your possession on your Zakat date.

Ref: Mufti Taqi Usmani
Commercial

Shop Inventory & Dead Stock

Zakat is calculated on the current retail value of all stock-in-trade. Furniture and machinery used in the shop are exempt.

Ref: Kitab al-Zakat
Commercial

Business Partnerships

Each partner pays Zakat on their share of liquid assets (Cash + Stock + Receivables) after deducting short-term debts.

Ref: Majmua Fatawa
Liabilities

Deducting Personal Loans

Money you owe can be subtracted. For long-term loans, only deduct the installments due for the next 12 months.

Ref: Islamic Fiqh Academy
Assets

Money Lent to Others

If repayment is expected, pay Zakat annually. If the person is likely to default, pay only once you actually receive the money.

Ref: Hidayah
Agriculture

Ushr on Seasonal Crops

Ushr (10%) is due on rain-watered crops. For tube-well watered crops, it is 5%. Due at every harvest.

Ref: Sahih Muslim
Livestock

Zakat on Farm Animals

Due on grazing animals if they reach Nisab (e.g. 40 goats). If kept for trade, 2.5% of total value is due.

Ref: Kitab al-Amwal
Income

Zakat on Monthly Salary

No Zakat on salary as it is received. Only the portion you save and hold until your Zakat date is included in wealth.

Ref: Fatawa Shami
Retirement

Provident Fund (PF)

Zakat is due on your own voluntary contributions annually. Employer's portion is taxed only upon receipt.

Ref: Contemporary Scholars
Wirasat

Zakat on Inherited Wealth

Once the inheritance is in your possession, the lunar cycle (Hawl) starts. Pay on your next Zakat anniversary.

Ref: Quran 4:11
Philanthropy

Zakat vs Sadaqah

Zakat is mandatory (2.5%) for specific categories. Sadaqah is voluntary and can be given to any person or cause.

Ref: Surah At-Tawbah
